1. Orientation

What's your previous experience on two wheels look like? If you're brand new to the scooting game, you’ll want to attend an in-person orientation. It’s a 30-minute session with a trained Scoot coach who will walk you through the whole thing. For those of you who have a motorcycle or moped license (M1 or M2), a lot of this may seem familiar to you already–take a look!

2. Find a scoot

To find a Scoot, use the Scoot app, which you can download for iPhone or Android, or just load Scoot from our website. With the app open, you’ll see see all available scoots nearby — reserve one! You’ve got 15 minutes of free reservation before billing starts.

Once you get to your scoot, turn it on and grab the keys from under the seat to unlock your handlebars.

4. Set up your gear

Once you've unlocked your scoot, it's time to get your gear ready to rock n' roll. There are three things you should do everytime you unlock your scoot.

  1. Unplug your scoot.If you’re riding from a powered garage, you'll hang the cord up on the Scoot Box. If you’re picking up from a Scoot Stop (on-street parking), there’s no charging so you’re all set!
  2. Put on your helmet. There are always two sizes of helmets in each scoot: a smaller and larger size. This is because everyone has a different sized head, and it’s important that our riders wear the right fit. The second helmet is NOT for a passenger — it’s against the law to carry someone else on your scoot.
  3. Dock your phone. Plug your phone into the cables and dock in on the top of the handlebars. While you’re scooting, your phone acts as your dashboard — showing your speed, directions, battery life, and distance.

5. Safety First!

Before you start riding, go through the pre-ride safety check, BLTB: check your battery, your lights, your tires, and your brakes. Remember: we inspect and maintain our scoots every week, but you’re about to get on the road, so check to make sure everything is in working order. If something’s not perfect, report it in the app and grab a different scoot. Remeber, BLTB!

6. Scoot Somewhere

Pick a new drop-off location in the app by tapping any red ‘P’. The app will show you scoot-specific directions, avoiding big hills, fast roads, and MUNI tracks. If you want to change your drop-off mid-ride, just tap any other red ‘P’. If you’re coming back to where you started, just keep your destination the same! Make sure to check on your battery, in the app, during your ride. Scoot’s go 20-25 miles on a single charge, which is plenty of range to get you all over SF. But do make sure you’ve got enough to get wherever you’re going.
